* Note regarding the averages per game played: The True North website does not include any "games played (GP)" data for the years 2005 – 2008; it only includes the goals, assists, points and PIMs during those years, and nothing before 2005 – useless fucks. Because of this, the number of games played for each player during 2005 – 2008 is assumed to be that player's average games played per year for the years 2009 and later. So the total games played over all seasons includes those assumed games played. This allows for the averaging of the stats to include those missing years.

This does, however, result in inflated figures for Hesketh in particular because although he played a full season in 2005 (and put up record numbers), no "games played" were counted. He then played in a single game in 2009. As a result, his stats per game over those two seasons are averaged over two games: his one officially recorded "game played" in 2009 and his assumed average of one game played in 2005. This is the only real outlier that I can see using this approach.

An alternate approach would be to exclude the 2005 – 2008 years from the averages/game, but then it wouldn't look right when you saw 100 games played, 100 goals total, and yet it displayed a goals per game of (say) 0.75 because 25 of those goals were scored between 2005 – 2008 and were therefore excluded from the average. (I realize there are other, non-mathematical reasons why those numbers wouldn't look right.)

I also note that Hesketh played a game with us 2 or 3 years ago, but that game is not reflected in the True North stats. We all know that that omission is not the only error or omission in the scoresheets. So let's not go trotting these stats off to Hockey News as reflecting reality too closely.

"inf" and "nan" mean infinity (or undefined), which results from a division by zero (because of the zero games in 2005 – 2008) and shows up for those players who only played in those years.
